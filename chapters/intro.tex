Unstable dynamical behavior is quite onipresent our world. For instance, if you try to balance a pencil on its tip, the side to which it falls is extremely sensitive on the initial position of the pencil. This unstable initial condition, however --- the one with the pencil standing perfectly on top of its tip --- is quite special if we constrast it to the set of \emph{all} the possible positions that the pencil could start in. Can you think of an example where this kind of unpredictable behavior is not the exception, but the default?
