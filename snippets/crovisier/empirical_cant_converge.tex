Here's why the item (iii) in prop 6.5 of~\cite{crovisierEmpiricalMeasuresPartially2020} cannot mean that the empirical measures
\begin{equation*}
  e_n(x) = \frac{1}{n}(\delta_{X_1(x)} + \cdots + \delta_{X_n(x)})
\end{equation*}
converge weakly to the Wiener measure \(W\), where
\begin{equation*}
  X_n(x)(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\sigma \cdot n}} \int_0^{nt} \phi(A^{\floor{s}}(x)) \;ds.
\end{equation*}
By contradiction, suppose \(e_n(x) \weakto W\) for \(m\)-almost every \(x \in \mathbb{T}^{2}\). We already know that \((X_n)_*m \weakto W\) from the weak invariance principle. Let
\begin{equation*}
  U = \{ f \in C[0,1] : \lVert f \rVert_{\infty} < 1/10 \}
\end{equation*}
which is an open set. By the forgery theorem, \(W(U) > 0\).
